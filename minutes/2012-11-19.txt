ST. LOUIS COUNTY LIBRARY DISTRICT
	MEETING OF THE BOARD
	HEADQUARTERS BRANCH
	MONDAY, NOVEMBER 19, 2012
	3:30 P.M.
	The monthly meeting of the Board of the St. Louis County Library District was held on Monday, November 19, 2012.  The agenda had been posted in all branches of the Library District at least 24 hours before the meeting.
	Attendance:
	Members present:
	Lynn Beckwith, President
	Edith Cunnane, Vice President via telephone
	Elena Kenyon, Treasurer
	Dr. Chingling Tai, Secretary
	Steve Sachs, Member
	ADMINISTRATOR'S AND GUESTS ATTENDING:
	Charles Pace, Director; Kristen Sorth, Asst. Director, Administration; Barbara Brain, Asst. Director, Adult and Support Services; Barbara Turkington, Asst. Director, Advancement; Gib Van Cleve, Manager, ITS; Marie Conlin, Planner; Eric Button, Community Services Administrator; Lisa Stump, Counsel, Lashly & Baer; Bill Winningham, Milliman, Mike Zwiener, Milliman Melissa Le, Lockton, and John Clempos, Compos Design.
	Others Attending:
	Kris Mooney, Mary Arnhart, Ed Clark,  Lori Boschert, Keir Haug,  Kathy Muller, Rachel Nowell, Stacy Berger, Trudy Williams, Aaron Eller, Emily Althoff,  Donna Spaulding, Laura Kasak, Erin Magner, Kimberly Clark, Julia Mangner, Michael Cohen, Paul Bayless, Cynthia Schultz, Anna Huckeby, Erin Chapman, Stephanie Johnson, Matt Lakin, Carrie Robb, Cheri Remington, Kathy Wiese, Gina Becker, Jennifer Alexander, Robbin Oehler, Anna Strackeljahn, Colleen Hall, Melissa Le, Jennifer McBride, Darryl Wallace, Anna Schurk, Joyce Loving, Dottie Wobbe and Sandra Williams.
	Approval of Minutes
	The Board approved the minutes of October 15, 2012 as presented (Sachs/Cunnane).
	Public Comment
	There was no public comment.
	Treasurer's Report
	The Library received  1st and 2nd quarter Missouri State Aid payments for fiscal year 2013.  The Federal Home Loan Mortgage Corporation Bond investment was     called on Oct. 19, 2012 generating $17,250 in interest. The Library received the first two E-rate discount payments for 2012/2013 for $152,919.
	The Annual staff in-service day was held October 8, 2012.  The cost will be reimbursed through the Abigail Harris fund at the end of the year.
	The Board approved the Treasurer's Report subject to audit (Cunnane/Kenyon).
	Director's Report
	Mr. Pace reported that online Ed2go is very popular among the customers. So far this year the Library has recorded more than 5.4 million visits and our programming attendance also continues to be strong.   Mr. Pace said he spoke to a number of media outlets and community groups within the last month.  We will have had 84 author events by the end of the year. Al
	Gore is coming Feb. 9th.
	The Winter Reading program has had a successful sign up. Dr. Tai would like to know results of completion.
	Roll out of the Prop L timeline will happen early next year to outline projects.
	Selection of bond counsel was Gilmore and Bell. We will be hiring an underwriter.
	Mr. Steve joined us on a warm Friday evening and drew a crowd of around 475 people. Though smaller than the first time he visited the Library, this is still a very good-sized crowd and shows his continuing popularity here in the St Louis area.
	Community Activities/Branch Visits
	During the past month Mr. Pace said he visited the Grand Glaize, Meramec Valley, Weber Road, Eureka Hills, and Cliff Cave branches. He spoke to the Jewish Community Relations Council, the Overland Business Association, and a tour group from Delta Dental.  He also addressed a meeting of school superintendents and administrators organized by the Cooperating School District. He had several complex interviews with various media outlets in the days leading up to the election.
	Facilities
	Mr. Pace said there was a very successful opening of the new Teen Center at Lewis and Clark on November 13. Wayne Hunter of the Rams was in attendance as was the football team and girls’ volleyball team from the Riverview Gardens School District. The new Rams-themed teen area is the eleventh such space to be opened in one of our branches. In other facilities news:
	·        The new roof at Indian Trails is complete
	·        Work will be getting underway soon on repairing the concrete plaza at Thornhill.
	·        The roof on Bridgeton Trails is nearing completion..
	.        Parking lot projects have been completed with the exception of striping at Florissant Valley and Grand Glaize.
	·        Work continues on the skylight replacements at Thornhill.
	·        The library logo will be applied to the new awnings at the Eureka Hills Branch in the near future.
	Vacation
	Mr. Pace said he would be on vacation from Tuesday, November 20 thru Friday, November 23 for the Thanksgiving holiday.
	Director’s Summit
	Mr. Pace said he would be attending a Director’s summit in Los Angeles on November 28-30. The summit is organized by Library Journal and is part of their ongoing effort to provide the latest information on the future direction of libraries in this country.
	Mr. Pace said there would be a number of programs coming up in the future including an event featuring former Vice President Al Gore.
	Old Business
	None
	New Business
	Library Board Policy 1 Bid Procedures (Other than Professional Services)
	The Library Board approved the revised Bid Procedures policy as written by the attorneys at Lashly & Baer (Tai/Kenyon).
	Lisa Stump has revised the procedure to grant authority to the Director or designee to purchase a large volume of
	small items (i.e., under $10.00) that may aggregate over $10,000.
	 2013 Structure Adjustments, Merit Raises and Compression Adjustments
	Melissa Lee, Lockton Co. addressed the Board regarding salary compression. 0-5%
	The  Library Board approved the a 0 - 5% merit raise range with a 3.5% average. These adjustments will cost $708,840.  Structure and compression adjustments based on the analysis conducted by Lockton will also take place.
	Motion by Edith Cunnane, second by Dr Chingling Tai.
	Roll Call:   Aye: Edith Cunnane, Dr. Chingling Tai, Elena Kenyon
	Nay: Steve Sachs, Lynn Beckwith
	 2013 Library Budget
	Kristen Sorth introduced Kris Mooney as the library's new Finance Manager. Tax rates were set in September at $0.1730 for Residential assessments, $ 0.20000 for Agricultural assessments, $ 0.1900 for Commercial assessments and $ 0.1500 for Personal Property assessments.  These rates will provide over 94% of the cash income to operate in  2013.  The Executive Summary on page 1 and the Revenue and Expenses on page 2 are a synopsis of the proposed 2013 Cash Budget.  The 2013 budget has a budgeted surplus of $5,000.
	Items listed in the budge will be presented to the Board when it's time for their implementation in 2013.
	Dr. Beckwith would like one person to be appointed in charge permanently in the director's absence.
	The Library Board approved of the 2013 budget (Cunnane/Tai).
	Adjourn
	Mr. Sachs made a motion to adjourn the meeting.  All approved by voice vote.
	Meeting adjourned at 4:42 p.m.
